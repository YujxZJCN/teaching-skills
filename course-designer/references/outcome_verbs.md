# Outcome Verb Guide (Bloom's Revised Taxonomy)

Working tables for `outcome_architect_agent` and assessment item writers. Levels per
Anderson & Krathwohl (2001).

## Verb tables by level

| Level | Cognitive demand | Strong verbs |
|-------|-----------------|--------------|
| remember | retrieve facts | define, list, recall, identify, state, label, recognize |
| understand | construct meaning | explain, summarize, classify, compare, interpret, paraphrase, illustrate, translate |
| apply | use in new situations | apply, implement, solve, compute, execute, demonstrate, use, operate |
| analyze | decompose & relate | analyze, differentiate, organize, deconstruct, attribute, diagnose, debug, test |
| evaluate | judge against criteria | evaluate, critique, justify, defend, assess, prioritize, recommend, validate |
| create | assemble something new | design, construct, formulate, compose, develop, propose, synthesize, derive |

## Banned operative verbs (not observable)

`know, understand, appreciate, be aware of, be familiar with, learn, grasp, realize,
believe, see, cover` — and "demonstrate an understanding of" (a disguise, not a fix).

## Rewrite patterns

| Weak | Diagnosis | Rewritten |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| "Understand sorting algorithms" | unobservable verb | "Select an appropriate sorting algorithm for a given workload and justify the choice in complexity terms" (evaluate) |
| "Know the major theories of motivation" | recall disguised as outcome | "Compare two motivation theories and apply each to a workplace case" (analyze/apply) |
| "Appreciate the role of statistics in research" | attitude, not capability | "Critique the statistical methods of a published study, identifying threats to validity" (evaluate) |
| "Demonstrate an understanding of supply and demand" | disguise verb | "Predict price and quantity effects of a market shock using supply-demand analysis" (apply) |

## Level-honesty checks

- The verb must match the **actual task demand**, not aspiration. "Evaluate the time
  complexity of an algorithm" is *apply/analyze* if students execute a known method on
  standard cases — "evaluate" in the Bloom sense means judgment against criteria with
  defensible reasoning.
- Conditions sharpen measurability: "...given an unfamiliar dataset", "...without
  references", "...to a non-specialist audience".
- One capability per outcome. "Analyze and present" = two outcomes (or one outcome with
  the presentation as the assessment vehicle, not the capability).

## Chinese verb equivalents (for bilingual syllabi)

记忆: 列举、识别、陈述 · 理解: 解释、概括、比较、阐释 · 应用: 运用、求解、演示、实现 ·
分析: 分析、区分、诊断、推断 · 评价: 评价、论证、评判、权衡 · 创造: 设计、构建、提出、综合

不可用作核心动词: 了解、掌握、熟悉、认识、体会、领会 (不可观测——同英文 banned 表)。
