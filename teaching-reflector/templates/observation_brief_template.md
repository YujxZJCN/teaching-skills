## Post-observation debrief agenda ({{debrief_date}})

Order matters: **descriptive before evaluative**, and the observed instructor
interprets first.

1. **Instructor's read first** (5 min) — how did the session compare to a typical one?
   Anything that went differently than planned?
2. **Observer describes, doesn't yet judge** (10 min) — what observably happened, tied
   to timestamps: "at minute 12, when you launched the group problem, I counted…"
   Counts and moments, not impressions.
3. **The watch-for requests** (10–15 min) — observer reports against each item from §3;
   instructor responds with intent ("what were you going for there?") before either
   evaluates.
4. **Evaluation only if invited** (5 min) — for formative observations, the observer's
   overall judgment is offered only if the instructor asks for it.
5. **One or two takeaways** (5 min) — instructor names what (if anything) they'll try;
   observer notes what they're stealing for their own teaching. More than two action
   items per observation is noise.
6. **Disposition of notes** — confirm: {{notes stay between us | instructor keeps them
   | summarized as triangulation evidence for a future eval-analysis run}}.
